The Calm

The lighthouse stood alone against the velvet dark, its beam sweeping across a sea as still as glass. The keeper watched from the gallery, counting the rhythm of the waves.

The Wind Rises

A breath of cold air from the north. The water began to whisper, then to speak. Whitecaps appeared like scattered teeth on the horizon, and the beam began to tremble.

The Storm

Thunder rolled across the black water. Waves rose like mountains, crashing against the stone with a fury that shook the very foundation. The light fought on, cutting through rain and spray.

The Eye

For a moment, silence. The storm held its breath. The keeper wiped salt from his eyes and saw, impossibly, stars through the thinning clouds. Then the wind returned, hungrier than before.

The Dawn

Gray light seeped into the world. The sea, exhausted, settled into a slow heave. The lighthouse stood, scarred but unbroken, its duty done until the next night fell.
